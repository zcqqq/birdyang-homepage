# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-05-30*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Bird & Yang is an Auckland-based integrated professional services firm combining legal, accounting, and consulting under one roof.
**What it does:** Provides coordinated legal, accounting, and consulting support for individuals, families, and businesses navigating complex cross-border and multi-disciplinary matters — particularly for Chinese-speaking clients moving between China and New Zealand.
**Product category:** Law firm / Professional services firm
**Product type:** Professional services (legal + accounting + consulting)
**Business model:** Fee-for-service (billable hours + fixed-fee engagements for immigration, property transactions, accounting)

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** SMEs with cross-border NZ-China operations, property investors, new immigrants establishing in NZ
**Decision-makers:** Business owners, property investors, families relocating to NZ, individuals needing immigration support
**Primary use case:** Chinese-speaking individuals/families/businesses who need coordinated legal + financial + consulting support for settling, investing, or operating in New Zealand
**Jobs to be done:**
- "Help me get the right visa and settle in New Zealand"
- "Handle my property purchase/development legally and tax-efficiently"
- "Set up and run my NZ business with proper legal and accounting structure"
**Use cases:**
- Immigration (skilled migrant, investor, work visas, residency)
- Property purchase/sale/development for overseas investors
- Business structuring and cross-border commercial matters
- Estate planning and trust setup for asset protection
- Ongoing accounting and tax compliance for NZ businesses
- Insurance and HR support for growing businesses

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Cross-border clients (especially Chinese-speaking) face fragmented professional services — separate lawyer, accountant, immigration adviser, insurance broker — with no coordination, communication gaps, and cultural/language barriers.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Separate firms don't talk to each other, creating gaps and duplicated effort
- Most NZ law firms lack Chinese language capability and cultural understanding
- Generic accountants don't understand the legal implications of cross-border structures
- Immigration advisers work in isolation from the legal and tax picture
**What it costs them:** Missed deadlines, compliance failures, suboptimal tax structures, unnecessary stress, higher total fees from duplicated work across multiple advisers.
**Emotional tension:** Uncertainty navigating unfamiliar NZ systems in a second language; fear of making costly mistakes with immigration, property, or business decisions; stress of coordinating multiple professionals who don't communicate.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Chen Legal, K3 Legal, Loo & Koo — Chinese-speaking Auckland law firms. Fall short because they offer legal only, not integrated accounting and consulting.
**Secondary:** Big 4 accounting firms + separate immigration advisers — comprehensive but expensive, impersonal, no cultural specialisation.
**Indirect:** DIY with online tools + community advice — free but high-risk for complex cross-border matters.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- True integration of legal + accounting + consulting (not just referral partnerships)
- Bilingual team (English + Mandarin) with deep understanding of both NZ and Chinese business/legal cultures
- Senior founding partners directly involved (Marshall Bird: 40+ years NZ law; Aimee Yang: China + NZ commercial expertise)
- China Desk for cross-border matters specifically
**How we do it differently:** One firm handles the legal structure, tax implications, immigration pathway, insurance, and HR — coordinated internally rather than across separate providers.
**Why that's better:** Clients get one point of contact, consistent advice, no gaps between disciplines, lower total cost, and faster resolution.
**Why customers choose us:** "I can explain my situation once, in Mandarin, and they handle everything — the visa, the company setup, the tax structure, the trust."

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Can a small firm really handle all these areas?" | Our team includes specialists in each discipline — integration means coordination, not generalism. Marshall has 40+ years in immigration/business law alone. |
| "How do your fees compare to using separate providers?" | Total cost is typically lower because we eliminate duplicate meetings, conflicting advice, and coordination overhead. |
| "I already have a lawyer/accountant" | We often work alongside existing advisers, or clients discover the efficiency of consolidation after trying both approaches. |

**Anti-persona:** Large corporates needing Big 4 audit services; pure litigation cases with no cross-disciplinary element; clients who only need the cheapest possible tax return.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Frustration with coordinating 3-4 separate professionals; language barriers with existing English-only advisers; getting conflicting advice from lawyer vs accountant.
**Pull:** "One firm that speaks my language and handles everything together"; the relief of not managing the coordination yourself.
**Habit:** Existing relationships with current lawyer/accountant; "better the devil you know"; reluctance to move files.
**Anxiety:** "Will a smaller firm have the same expertise?"; "What if the integration means less specialised attention?"; cost uncertainty.
